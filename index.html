Researches

Our research areas are diverse yet interconnected deeply with each other, the success of one research depends on the progress of the others. We believe that the combination of those aspects of science will lead to paradigm shift in all fields of science.

Common Brain

Mission
Foundation
The establishment of the scientific foundation and technical methodology for achieving synthetic intelligence in a cloud architecture.
Framework
The establishment of a framework that facilitate the abstraction of fundamental logic and the extraction of essential knowledge.
Community
The establishment of a framework for crowdsourcing empirical experience.
Vision
Deploying the common brain across the world and providing intelligence as a service in every aspect of technology and hoping to achieve perfect harmony by bridging the technological differences between different technological stacks.
Architecture
The architecture of the common brain can be achieved with a group of intelligent independent elements that separate concerns and fulfil fundamental functional objectives.
Process Definition
  • DNA
    which is a configuration file that holds the logic and fundamentals of an application.
  • Embryogenesis
    which is the process of turning generic nodes into functional applications, that consumes a DNA file and results in an Embryo object.
  • Neurogenesis
    which is a process that abstracts the utilization of resources and provides resources to applications via resource allocation, and results in a Neuron object.
  • Embryo
    which is the initial stage of a functional prototype of the application and is formed via Embryogenesis, and consists of all essential dependencies for the application. (Stage I - Development)
  • Fetus
    which is the second stage in the Embryogenesis, and forms a prototype of the application with the addition of basic autonomic, sensory and somatic functionality. (Stage II - Development)
  • Neonate
    which is a deployed functional application ready for production (Stage III - Production)
Object Definition
  • Nuclei
    (Generic Node) which is the initial DNA application, and forms the first element of the common brain.
  • Neuron
    (Resource Node) which is a single unit of resource provided by the Neurogenesis.
  • Brain
    (Central Control Node) which is the central control node for the application, and is an essential part of every embryo.
  • Spinal
    (Communication InterNode) which is the communication internode for the application, and is an essential part of every embryo.
  • Autonomic
    (Autonomic Node) which is a node that executes instructions for the central control node.
  • Somatic
    (Somatic Node) which is a node that provides a layer that separates concern for the output stream.
  • Sensory
    (Sensory Node) which is a node that provides a layer that separates concern for the input stream.

Human Extensibility

Mission
Foundation
The establishment of the scientific ground for a field of science concerned with the study of the physics and physiology of the human being, and the techniques and algorithms for human extensibility via DNA mutation through natural means.
Framework
The establishment of a framework that facilitates the comprehension of human extensibilty in real life applications.
Community
The initiation of research in the field of human extensibility and inspiring the scientific community to contribute to the advancement of the human race.
Vision
Educating people about human extensibility in order to achieve a better understanding of the human body and how it functions. Introducing natural techniques that has a positive effect on the human genetic qualities via DNA mutation in aspects such as vital function efficiency, semantic processing, spatial awareness, kinetic abilities and cell recovery. Hoping that improvements in the genetic qualities of the current generation will lead to superior next generations, with better genetic qualities such as improved health and better immune system, advanced semantic processing, advanced spatial processing, advanced kinetic abilities and better resilience.
Architecture
Human extensibility is the study of the physics and physiology of the human being, and the techniques and algorithms for human extensibility via DNA mutation through natural means.
Process Definition
  • Physiology
    The scientific study of the normal functions of each object.
  • Physics
    The scientific study of environmental effects on each object.
  • Algorithms
    Measurements of the impact of environmental effects on the normal functionality of each object.
  • Techniques
    Solutions which utilize natural techniques to extend the regular functionality of each object.
Object Definition
  • Autonomic
    which is concerned with extensibility of the vital system functions of the human body.
  • Brain
    which is concerned with extensibility of the brain cognitive and psychological functions.
  • Somatic
    which is concerned with extensibility in motor functions.
  • Sensory
    which is concerned with extensibility in sensory perception.
  • Recovery
    which is concerned with techniques for efficient resilience and better mutation.
